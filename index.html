My Projects

This webpage is designed to give you a better idea of the projects that I have worked on over the years and the skills that I have developed in the process. All of the projects that I have completed have a green header while all of the projects that I am still working on have a yellow header. By clicking on a project's card you can get a more detailed description of it, as well as a list of the skills that I employed, and various other information.

Engineering Projects

FSAE: Charging Cart

I designed and manufactured the charging cart that houses the charging unit and all charging circuitry for the electric vehicle of the Columbia Formula SAE team. It will also house the accumulator when it is being charged.

FSAE: Main Low Voltage Board Updates

I worked with one partner from the Low Voltage team of the Columbia Formula SAE club to update the schematics and PCB design of the main low voltage board. We also created a bill of materials, ordered the pieces, and will begin assembly soon.

FSAE: Shutdown Circuit Testing

I designed this PCB to be able to test the Shutdown Circuit and Brake System Plausibility Device of the Columbia Formula SAE team's EV independently from the rest of the vehicle using switches, potentiometers, and LED indicators.

Robotics MATE ROV Frame

I designed and assembled the frame of the Columbia University Robotics Club's robot for the MATE ROV competition. The frame is made from high density polyethylene.

Robotics MATE ROV Thruster Guards

I designed and assembled the thruster guards of the Columbia University Robotics Club's robot for the MATE ROV competition. The guards were 3D printed with an Ultimaker printer.

RC Car Speedometer

I built a speedometer for an RC vehicle using an Arduino, infrared sensor, and an OLED display. The OLED display was placed in front of a POV camera that I mounted on the vehicle.

Watch Explanation on YouTube

Toilet Paper Holder CAD Design

I designed a toilet paper holder on SolidWorks from scratch for the midterm project of my Computer Graphics and Design class. The assignment was to create a model of any item found in our house making use of as many techniques as we could.

Space Race! Math Game CAD Design

I worked in a team of three in my Computer Graphics and Design class to come up with a fun, handheld game that would teach young children simple algebra and then modeled it on Solidworks.

Art of Engineering Final: Wack-A-Bobcat

I worked with a team of 5 students to come up with, design, and build a game for the final assignment of my Art of Engineering class at Columbia University. I was responsible for creating the CAD design of the housing box and the circuitry for the Arduino.
